Detailed projects

Three active research initiatives with marine acoustic depth.

Ibiza · Formentera

Posidonia Soundscapes: Conservation & Music, Ibiza

A multidisciplinary project that blends underwater acoustic monitoring with artistic storytelling. The research documents the soundscape of Posidonia oceanica meadows, characterizing both biological and human-made noise and translating it into formats that raise awareness about those habitats.

Methodology includes stationary hydrophone deployments, spectral analysis of noise sources, and acoustic indices that reflect biodiversity and habitat condition. The project also develops listening experiences and outreach activities to bring marine soundscapes nearer to local communities and international audiences.

Partners: local conservation bodies, music producers, the United Nations Decade of Ocean Science.

La Caleta

Pools as sound shelters

This research line studies the acoustic ecology of intertidal rock pools and their role as refuges for small invertebrates and juvenile fish. By measuring sound levels and acoustic habitat quality, the work explores how microhabitats function under human pressure.

The field protocol includes repeated recordings across tidal cycles, comparative analysis of exposed versus sheltered pools, and assessment of anthropogenic noise from nearby coastal activities. Results inform nature-based coastal management and species protection.

Impact: improved understanding of coastal acoustic niches, support for habitat restoration, and new evidence for low-impact marine planning.

Strait of Gibraltar

SEANIMALMOVE

SEANIMALMOVE combines acoustic monitoring with movement ecology to track marine vertebrates in one of the busiest marine passages in Europe. The project aims to understand how cetaceans and coastal species adapt to intense traffic, noise and environmental change.

The approach uses passive acoustic monitoring arrays, animal-borne sensors, and data fusion with visual surveys, offering a richer picture of seasonal dynamics and behaviour. Acoustic detections are correlated with shipping noise, sonar activity and ecological events.

Outcome: more robust risk assessments for marine traffic, better conservation planning, and acoustic indicators for habitat resilience in the Strait.
